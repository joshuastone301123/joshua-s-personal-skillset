When assembling the **🎵 Songs** section of the weekly NYC dance-music discovery briefing, expand the source pool and cross-reference:

- Pull candidate tracks from **Spotify playlists** (editorial + curated dance/house/techno playlists relevant to Josh's taste — e.g. mint, Friday Cratediggers, Housewerk, plus curator/label-run playlists).
- Pull candidate tracks from **Beatport Top 100s** across the relevant genre charts (house, tech house, melodic house & techno, minimal / deep tech, afro house, etc. — pick the charts that match the week's drift, not all of them).
- **Cross-reference everything**: prefer tracks that show up across multiple signals (e.g. on Beatport Top 100 AND in a curated Spotify playlist, or charting + label-co-signed + DJ-supported). A track surfacing in one source is a candidate; a track surfacing in two or more is a stronger pick. Use overlap as the de-facto quality filter.

**Why:** Josh wants the songs section grounded in what's actually moving in the scene right now — not just what I happen to surface from press/reviews. Beatport charts and Spotify playlists both reflect real DJ/curator behavior; intersecting them is a sharper signal than either alone.

**How to apply:**
- Add Spotify playlist sweeps and Beatport Top 100 sweeps to the song-discovery step of every weekly compile.
- Before finalizing the 5 song picks, build a quick overlap pass: which candidates appear in 2+ sources? Lead with those.
- Single-source picks are still allowed when the track is clearly remarkable, but the default should be cross-referenced.
- Format of the songs section itself is unchanged — still `Artist — [Track](spotify-url)`, per [[reference-weekly-discovery-telegram-delivery]].
